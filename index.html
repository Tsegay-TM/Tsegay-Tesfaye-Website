About Me

Bridging Research and Industry

I am an Associate Professor and engineering leader with over 17 years of experience at the intersection of research, teaching, and industrial practice in industrial and systems engineering.

My work centers on building automatically controlled manufacturing systems aligned with the requirements of the Factory of the Future, addressing both scientific and industrial challenges in intelligent manufacturing, automatic control, optimization, and lean manufacturing. Technology transfer and reverse engineering are complementary themes of my research.

I have led internationally funded projects, delivered high-value industrial consultancy across manufacturing, metals, pharmaceuticals, and infrastructure, supervised doctoral and masters research, and published in leading peer-reviewed journals — while holding senior academic leadership roles as Dean and Director.
